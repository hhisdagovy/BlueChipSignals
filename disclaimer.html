Risk Factors & Trading Losses

Trading securities, options, futures, ETFs, cryptocurrencies, or any other financial instruments involves substantial risk, including the possible loss of your entire invested capital. The following risk factors, among others, may result in severe adverse outcomes:

  • Market volatility - Prices of financial instruments can move rapidly and unpredictably.
  • Liquidity risk - You may be unable to exit a position at a desired price due to insufficient market volume.
  • Execution risk - Slippage, order delays, or platform outages may cause trades to execute at unfavorable prices.
  • Leverage risk - The use of margin or leveraged instruments can amplify both gains and losses.
  • Counterparty risk - The insolvency or default of a broker, exchange, or counterparty may result in the loss of assets.
  • Regulatory and legal risk - Changes in tax law, regulations, or government policy may adversely affect markets or your specific positions.
  • Technology risk - System failures, data errors, or cybersecurity incidents may cause financial harm.
  • Macroeconomic risk - Geopolitical events, interest rate changes, inflation, or economic recessions may move markets adversely.
